**Ontario auto insurance changes 2026** give drivers more choice, but a lower premium can now mean less income and family protection after a crash. From July 1, several accident benefits became optional for new policies. Existing policyholders generally keep their selections at renewal unless they opt out. Before chasing a small saving, compare it with the benefit you would need if an injury stopped you from working or caring for someone.

*Published July 18, 2026. This article provides general information, not insurance or legal advice. Policy wording, eligibility and claim decisions depend on individual facts.*

## Quick answer: “optional” does not mean unimportant

The [Financial Services Regulatory Authority of Ontario](https://www.fsrao.ca/consumers/auto-insurance/purchasing-your-policy/what-standard-auto-insurance-policy) says medical, rehabilitation and attendant-care benefits remain mandatory in the accident-benefit package. Benefits such as income replacement, caregiver, non-earner, death and funeral, dependent care, indexation and some additional expenses can be optional.

Third-party liability remains mandatory, with a $200,000 minimum. Other required parts of the standard policy also remain. The reform changes choices within the package; it does not make driving uninsured lawful.

That trade-off is the core of **Ontario auto insurance changes 2026**: drivers can tailor more of the policy, but they also carry more responsibility for identifying gaps. ## Ontario auto insurance changes 2026: 7 checks before opting out

| Check | Question to answer | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Employment | Would work benefits replace enough income? | Shortfalls still have to be paid |
| Family | Does anyone depend on your care or earnings? | Optional benefits protect more than the driver |
| New vs renewal | Is this a brand-new policy? | Defaults differ |
| Named people | Who receives selected optional coverage? | Not every passenger gets every option |
| Savings | What is the exact annual reduction? | A monthly number can hide the trade-off |
| Other insurance | Is coverage truly equivalent? | Definitions, limits and waiting periods differ |
| Documentation | Is the selection shown in writing? | Coverage on the crash date controls |

### 1. Separate a new policy from a renewal

For a new policy bought on or after July 1, the minimum accident-benefit package starts narrower. At renewal, current customers retain existing accident benefits unless they choose to remove an optional one. Do not assume your neighbour's default is yours; read the declarations and benefit-selection form.

### 2. Test income replacement against a real disability scenario

If you remove income replacement, calculate what employer sick leave, disability insurance, savings and a spouse's income would cover. Check waiting periods, maximums, taxes and how long benefits last. “I have benefits at work” is not enough unless you know the amount and exclusions.

### 3. Consider the people attached to the policy

The [Insurance Bureau of Canada](https://www.ibc.ca/issues-and-advocacy/auto-insurance/ontario-auto-insurance-changes) explains that chosen optional benefits generally apply to the named insured, spouse, dependants and listed drivers. Other occupants may receive only mandatory benefits. Ask how your household and frequent drivers are defined.

### 4. Compare annual savings with the protection removed

[CityNews](https://toronto.citynews.ca/2026/06/15/auto-insurance-changes-ontario-drivers/) quoted one broker estimating savings of about $7 to $20 monthly when protections are removed. That is an example, not a market-wide promise. Obtain a written quote both ways and compare the annual difference with the weekly income benefit or lump-sum protection at stake.

### 5. Do not confuse accident benefits with vehicle damage

Accident benefits help people after injury. Collision, comprehensive and direct compensation for property damage address different losses. Ontario has allowed drivers to decline direct compensation property damage since 2024 under specified conditions. Removing one coverage does not make another one replace it.

For financed vehicles, a lender may also require physical-damage coverage under the loan contract. **Ontario auto insurance changes 2026** do not cancel those contractual requirements. High-risk borrowing secured by a vehicle carries separate consequences, outlined in our [title-loan guide](/blog/title-loans-canada-bad-credit).

### 6. Recheck after life changes

A new job, lost workplace benefits, marriage, a dependent child or a new listed driver can change the calculation. Optional benefits can generally be changed, but only the coverages in force on the accident date matter. Set a yearly review reminder rather than treating the first selection as permanent.

### 7. Get the explanation in writing

Ask the licensed agent or broker to show the premium with and without each option, who is covered, the maximum benefit and key exclusions. Keep the policy documents and selection confirmation. If wording is unclear, ask again before agreeing.

## A safer renewal conversation

Use these questions:

1. Which benefits are currently on my policy?
2. Which will remain automatically at renewal?
3. What exact annual amount would each removal save?
4. Who in my household receives the optional benefit?
5. What workplace or private coverage could overlap?
6. What happens if I cannot work for six months?
7. When does any change take effect?

The safest use of **Ontario auto insurance changes 2026** is a documented comparison, not a one-click effort to produce the lowest displayed premium.

## Bottom line

**Ontario auto insurance changes 2026** can produce a more customized policy, but customization transfers more decision-making to the driver. Confirm the dollars saved, the people protected and the income risk retained before removing a benefit that may be hard to replace after a collision.
